Around 700 million people suffer today from water scarcity in the world.

In the World 43 countries suffer today from water scarcity.

By 2025, 1.8 billion people will be living in countries or regions with absolute water scarcity, and two-thirds of the world's population could be living under water stressed conditions.

How to prevent Water scarcity.

This is all about using water more efficiently. Simple things like taking shorter showers, fixing leaky faucets, and using water-saving appliances can make a big difference. In agriculture, using drip irrigation and choosing drought-resistant crops can conserve significant amounts of water.

ONE

Reducing water use

Building dams and reservoirs can store water during wet seasons for use during dry periods. Rainwater harvesting is another technique, where rainwater is collected from rooftops or other surfaces and stored for later use.

TWO

Increasing water storage

This involves better infrastructure to reduce leaks in pipes and canals. It also includes investing in new technologies like desalination, which removes salt from seawater to make it usable.

THREE

Improving water management

Pollution of rivers, lakes, and groundwater can make them unusable. Proper wastewater treatment and reducing pollution from industries and agriculture are crucial steps. Healthy wetlands also play a vital role in filtering water naturally.

FOUR

Protecting water sources

Educating people about the importance of water conservation and encouraging them to adopt water-saving practices is essential. This can be done through public awareness campaigns, educational programs, and community initiatives.
